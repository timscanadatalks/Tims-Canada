How Canadian Fast-Food Menus Are Structured for Different Dayparts

If you've ever rushed into a Tim Hortons at 3 PM hoping for a breakfast sandwich (only to find out it's past the cutoff), or wondered why McDonald's Canada keeps Egg McMuffins going strong all day ‚Äî you're seeing dayparting at work.

Dayparting means dividing the day into specific time slots (breakfast, lunch, afternoon snack, dinner, late-night), each with targeted menu items, pricing, and promotions. Canadian fast-food chains are masters at this because of our unique coffee culture, harsh winters, and love for quick comfort food.

In this guide, we'll break down how major chains like Tim Hortons, McDonald's Canada, A&W, and others structure their menus around these dayparts, why they do it, and what it means for you as a customer.

Reasons for daypart-specific menus:
